UBEC vs wedge PCB — comparison memo Minimal · honest · take to bench

Goal: Measure the same wedge PSU twice: once UBEC-modded, then after swapping to your PCB (same transformer, harness, enclosure where practical). Report what the data shows — not which side “wins” by default.

Honesty: Only claim what this session supports. If results are close, say comparable under these conditions. Note PCB revision and case open vs closed.

What a UBEC is (here): RC-style small DC‑DC buck (often 5 V) on a tiny PCB — SMD IC, inductor, often heat-shrink or potting. Wedge mods feed it from the rectified DC off the original transformer. There is no TO‑220 tab and no bolt-on heatsink like your LM2576 + HS1. This memo compares electrical behaviour first (V, ripple); thermals are not symmetric between the two.

0 — Before you start

  • ☐ Photo or sketch of probe points (same for both DUTs), e.g. +5 V / GND at C64 connector.
  • ☐ Record: date, ambient °C, mains (e.g. 230 V / 50 Hz), scope model, probe (1× / 10×), AC + 20 MHz BW + peak detect — same for both runs.
  • ☐ E-load and cabling path (e.g. harness → connector → pigtail to load).

PCB build: Note KiCad / git revision or BOM date; “current board” vs “boards when they arrive.” If Phase B uses a different PCB rev, disclose it in the results table.

2 — Phase B: Your PCB (after swap)

  • Remove UBEC; install your PCB — same transformer, harness, e-load wiring.
  • Repeat A1–A4 with identical scope settings and same probe points.
  • Note case open vs closed the same way for both phases (airflow affects UBEC module and your board).

Temperature — not a matched UBEC vs PCB contest: UBECs have no LM2576 tab; heat is in the SMD chip / inductor, often under shrink or potting — a thermocouple is awkward and brand-dependent. Skip °C on the UBEC unless you have a repeatable method (e.g. IR on exposed surface). For your PCB only (optional): bead on LM2576 tab into HS1, wires under heatsink screw (same as measurement-procedure-and-report.html) — documents your build, not a °C-for-°C UBEC comparison.
